Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Bangladesh 2.0 drives innovation by leveraging cutting-edge technologies to create smart solutions across governance, education, healthcare, and infrastructure, transforming the nation into a knowledge-based economy.

Sustainability

Bangladesh 2.0 emphasizes sustainability through smart technology integration, fostering green development, efficient resource use, and resilience to environmental challenges for a prosperous future.

Infrastructure

The infrastructure of Bangladesh 2.0 focuses on building smart, tech-driven systems, including modernized transportation, energy-efficient urban planning, and digital connectivity to support sustainable development.

Empowerment

Bangladesh 2.0 drives empowerment by leveraging technology to enhance education, create inclusive economic opportunities, and ensure equal access to digital resources for all citizens.


The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Biulding state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

"The future scope of Bangladesh 2.0 focuses on transforming the nation into a smart, sustainable, and technologically advanced economy. With initiatives in sectors like smart cities, digital governance, and green technologies, it aims to create a robust infrastructure and a knowledge-driven economy. Emphasizing innovation, education, and healthcare, Bangladesh 2.0 is set to empower the youth and build a digital workforce to compete globally. This transformation will contribute to achieving the country’s goal of becoming a high-income nation by 2041."

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh
